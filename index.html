Description and Goals

CPSs are systems that combine continuous and discrete elements, providing a comprehensive modeling approach to various scientific and engineering issues. They apply to a wide range of fields, such as embedded systems, robotics, real-time software, power grids, transportation systems, process control, green building, mobile health, and biological systems, among others.

The course will begin by revisiting fundamental concepts that are essential to CPS, such as the diverse applications of CPS in different fields, and the latest technological advancements, such as wireless sensor networks, RFID, mobile computing, and mesh networks. Following that, the course will delve into wireless sensor networks, real-time systems, and hybrid system modeling, analysis and design. The class will present wireless sensor networking technology, as well as scheduling algorithms and protocols for various networks, in addition to application-oriented material. The course will conclude with the presentation of novel CPS research and emerging topics in CPS applications.

The goal of the course is to provide students with the necessary foundations to apply wireless sensor networking, scheduling theory, and algorithms in the field of CPS.

Course Subjects (tentative)

This course covers the unfolding research challenges and directions in distributed sensor networks and cyber-physical systems, discusses common misconceptions, presents the underlying theoretical foundations, and sheds light on related recent standards and embedded system technologies. The class is primarily paper-reading. It is divided into four main parts;

1.   a brief introduction to distributed cyber-physical systems,

2.   a description of emerging applications,

3.   a list of major distributed services and underlying foundations, and

We may cover the following detailed topics,

1)    Review: Continuous systems theory

2)    Review: Discrete event systems

3)    Modeling frameworks and systems properties

4)    Modeling and simulation tools

5)    Introduction to wireless sensor networks

6)    Introduction to real time scheduling

7)    Introduction to hybrid systems

8)    Controller design and synthesis

9)    Scheduling in control systems
